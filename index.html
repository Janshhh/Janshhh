About Me

There's two things I love: science and stories. The world is full of magic, and scientists are the mages of the modern age. Why is the sky blue? Why are leaves green? What are those lights in the sky at night? Science will provide us with the answers, and ways to use this natural magic to our advantage.

Being fascinated by how the world works, how the universe works, I spent ten years taking a deep dive into physics. I completed my PhD in condensed matter physics at the University of Amsterdam in May 2022. However, exploring the depths of one subject also has some downsides. My horizon felt too narrow, I missed thinking about the things outside my specific research field, and I wanted to share more magic with people outside of academia.

Since then, I've made a career out of what used to be my hobby: popular science writing, editing, and storytelling. I work as an editor for the European Space Agency's science missions, and write freelance science stories for newspapers and research institutes.
